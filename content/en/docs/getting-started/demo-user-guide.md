# Welcome to STING! üêù

You've been granted access to a live STING demo server. This guide will walk you through your first login and some fun things to try.

## Logging In

### Step 1: Check Your Email

You should have received a **magic link** email from STING. Click the link to set up your account.

> **No email?** Check your spam folder. The email comes from the STING server and might get filtered.

### Step 2: Set Up Your Passkey

After clicking the magic link, you'll be prompted to create a **passkey**. This is your secure, passwordless login method.

- On **Mac/Windows**: Your browser will offer to save the passkey to your device or password manager (1Password, iCloud Keychain, etc.)
- On **Mobile**: You can use Face ID, Touch ID, or your device PIN

> **Tip:** Passkeys are phishing-resistant and more secure than passwords. No more remembering complex strings!

### Step 3: You're In!

Once your passkey is set up, you'll land on the STING dashboard. Welcome to the hive! üçØ

## Things to Try

### üêù Chat with Bee

Bee is your AI assistant that knows about STING and can help answer questions.

1. Click **"Bee Chat"** in the sidebar
2. Try asking something like:
   - *"What is STING and what can it do?"*
   - *"How do Honey Jars work?"*
   - *"Help me understand the security features"*
3. Notice how Bee pulls context from the knowledge base to give informed answers

**Pro tip:** Click the **Honey Jar selector** (üçØ icon) to connect Bee to specific knowledge bases for more focused answers.

---

### üìä Generate a Report

STING can generate AI-powered reports on topics you're researching.

1. Go to **"Report Bee"** in the sidebar
2. Click **"Generate Report"**
3. Enter a prompt like:
   - *"Create a comparison of traditional document management vs AI-powered knowledge platforms"*
   - *"Analyze best practices for organizing enterprise security documentation"*
4. Watch the progress bar as STING researches, writes, and quality-checks your report
5. Download the finished PDF!

> **Behind the scenes:** Your report goes through ReviewBee quality assurance before delivery.

---

### üçØ Explore Honey Jars

Honey Jars are STING's knowledge containers. Think of them as smart folders that understand their contents.

1. Click **"Honey Jars"** in the sidebar
2. Browse the available jars (some are pre-loaded with STING documentation)
3. Click a jar to see its documents
4. Try the **"Query with Bee"** button to ask questions about that specific jar's contents
